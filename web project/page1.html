Dr. Ahmed Zewail

"Father Of Femto-Chemistry"

Biography

Born on February 26, 1946

Ahmed Zewail was an esteemed Egyptian-American scientist, recognized for his groundbreaking work in the field of chemistry and particularly for his contributions to femtochemistry, which involves studying chemical reactions on extremely short timescales (femtoseconds, or quadrillionths of a second).

Education

Zewail earned his bachelor's and master's degrees from Alexandria University before pursuing his Ph.D. at the University of Pennsylvania in the United States. He later became a naturalized citizen of the United States.

Awards And Honors

His most notable achievement came in 1999 when he won the Nobel Prize in Chemistry for his pioneering work in femtochemistry. Using ultrafast laser techniques, Zewail was able to observe and document the behavior of molecules during extremely rapid chemical reactions, providing invaluable insights into the dynamics of chemical reactions at the molecular level.

Aside from his scientific accomplishments, Zewail was also a prominent advocate for science education and research in the Middle East and globally. He held several prestigious positions, including serving as a professor at the California Institute of Technology.

Sadly, Ahmed Zewail passed away on August 2, 2016, leaving behind a remarkable legacy in the world of science and education.
