Dr. Ali Mostafa Mosharafa

Biography

born on July 11, 1898 in the city of Damietta

Dr. Ali Mostafa Mosharafa was the eldest son of Mustafa Musharrafa, one of the notables and wealthy people of that city, and those who excelled in religious sciences who were influenced by the ideas of Jamal al-Din al-Shaabi and Muhammad Abduh al-Qawwaniyah in understanding Islam and combating the creativity and superstitions of religion. He was one of the diligent scholars. In, and among his followers and disciples is His Highness (the owner of the fifth doctrine).

Youth And Education

He received his first lessons from his mother and then at the “Ahmed Al-Ketbi” school, and he was always one of the first to study, but his childhood was devoid of all its joys. Where he says about it: “As a child, I was desperate to be in the field, so my childhood was devoid of all joy. In those years, I learned that playing a game is a waste of time (as his mother used to say), and I learned dignity and serenity in the age of fun and enjoyment, and even running I considered it a departure from dignity.” His father died on January 8, 1910. After losing his fortune in cotton speculation in 1907, he lost his land, money, and even his home. With the death of the father, the son, Ali, who was not yet ten years old, became the head of his family, and after that from his second mother and his brothers, Nafisa, Mustafa, Attia, and Hassan, he took over the supervision of the family and moved to Cairo with their maternal grandmother, where they rented an apartment in the Muhyi Bek neighborhood in Abdeen. While there is no Ali at the Abbasid Secondary School in Alexandria, which has a year in the free boarding section; He moved to the Saidia School in Cairo, also for free, due to his academic excellence. He was examined in the first section, including secondary school (aptitude) in 1912, and in the second section (baccalaureate) in 1914. He was ranked second in the entire Egyptian country and from the age of six months, which is a unique event in The world of education in Egypt at that time, and this excellence (especially in scientific subjects) qualified him to enroll in any higher school of his choice, such as medicine or science, but he preferred to enroll in the Higher School, from which he graduated after three years with first rank, so the Ministry of Education chose him to go to scientific school. Britain at its expense.

Achievements

Mosharafa obtained his primary certificate in 1910 ranking second nationwide. He obtained his Baccalaureate at the age of 16, becoming the youngest student at that time to be awarded such a certificate, and again ranking second. He preferred to enroll in the Teachers' College rather than the faculties of Medicine or Engineering due to his deep interest in mathematics. He graduated in 1917. Due to his excellence in mathematics, the Egyptian Ministry of Education sent him to England where he obtained a BSc (Honors) from the University of Nottingham in 1920. The Egyptian University consented to grant Mosharafa another scholarship to complete his doctoral thesis. During his stay in London, he was published many times in prominent science magazines. He obtained a PhD in 1923 from King's College London in the shortest possible time permissible according to the regulations there. In 1924 Mosharafa was awarded the degree of Doctor of Science, the first Egyptian and 11th scientist in the entire world to obtain such a degree.

Death

He died on January 15, 1950 from a heart attack, and there is doubt as to how he died; It is believed that he died of poisoning or that one of King Farouk's delegates was behind his death. It is also believed that it was one of the operations of the Israeli Mossad. However, Dr. Ali Mustafa Mosharafa’s book: A Wealth the World Lost, written by his brother Dr. Attiya Mosharafa, completely denies these rumors and confirms that he died in his bed.
